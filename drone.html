Autonomous Aerial Object Retrieval

This project explores the limits of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models in non-static, high-vibration environments. Our goal is to enable an aerial robot to execute complex retrieval tasks based on natural language commands (e.g., "Pick up the red block").

Hardware-Software Co-Design

Integrating an SO101 arm onto a lightweight drone presented a significant mechatronic challenge. I worked to optimize the Thrust-to-Weight ratio while maintaining enough Edge Compute power to run inference locally.

  • Edge Computing: Integrated an NVIDIA Jetson Nano to handle real-time vision processing and SmolVLA inference.
  • Dynamic Stability: Problem-solved the center-of-gravity (CoG) shifts caused by the movement of a heavy robotic arm relative to the drone's chassis.
  • Power Management: Balanced high-capacity battery requirements with the strict weight constraints of an aerial platform.
